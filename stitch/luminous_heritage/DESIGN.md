## Brand & Style

This design system embodies an **Artisan-Editorial** aesthetic, blending the raw, tactile beauty of Indian craftsmanship with the sophisticated restraint of global luxury. The brand identity is defined by "Indian Roots, Global Dreams," focusing on the quiet luxury found in the intersection of nature and light.

The visual direction follows a **Modern-Minimalist** approach with **Brutalist** layout tendencies—using asymmetric grids and overlapping elements to create a sense of dynamic movement. The style evokes an emotional response of warmth, exclusivity, and intellectual curiosity. It treats each product not as a commodity, but as a "sculpture" with a narrative history.

Key stylistic pillars include:
- **Sophisticated Darkness:** A canvas of deep charcoals that allows the "Heritage Glow" to take center stage.
- **Asymmetric Narrative:** Non-traditional grid structures that mimic the unpredictable beauty of wood grain.
- **Material Storytelling:** High-quality imagery and generous whitespace that prioritize the "fingerprint of nature" over decorative UI.

## Colors

The palette is anchored in a **Dark Mode** environment, utilizing a spectrum of "Rich Blacks" and "Deep Charcoals" to create a cinematic backdrop. This intentional darkness serves to maximize the visual impact of the "Signature Glow."

- **Primary (Amber/Gold):** Represents the light passing through pinewood. Used for interactive accents, critical CTAs, and luminous highlights.
- **Secondary (Wood Tones):** Warm, earthy mid-tones derived from mature pinewood grain. Used for subtle dividers and secondary containers.
- **Neutral (Blacks/Grays):** A tiered system of near-blacks to provide depth without losing the "void" effect.
- **Luminous White:** Reserved strictly for high-contrast typography and essential icons to ensure legibility against dark backgrounds.

## Typography

The typographic system relies on a high-contrast pairing that mirrors the "Artisan/Modern" brand split.

- **Display & Headlines:** Use **Playfair Display**. This serif typeface provides the "luxury editorial" feel. Large display sizes should use tighter letter spacing for a modern, high-fashion impact.
- **Body Copy:** Use **Hanken Grotesk**. This sans-serif offers a clean, neutral, and highly readable counterpoint to the expressive headlines. It ensures that technical "Material Research" text feels scientific and precise.
- **UI Labels:** Use **Space Grotesk**. Its geometric and slightly technical nature suits navigation and interactive labels, especially when set in all-caps with generous tracking.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout philosophy is based on an **Asymmetric Editorial Grid**. Instead of a rigid column structure, elements are placed to create a rhythmic flow that guides the eye through "Storytelling Layers."

- **Grid Model:** A 12-column fluid grid is used as a base, but imagery and text blocks frequently "break the grid" or overlap one another to create depth.
- **Whitespace:** Generous section gaps (160px+) are mandatory to preserve the "Quiet Luxury" feel. 
- **Layering:** Use negative margins (element-overlap) to stack images over text or vice-versa, creating a tactile, "collaged" feel inspired by high-end design journals.
- **Adaptation:** On mobile, the layout collapses into a single-column focused on vertical storytelling, with overlaps reduced to maintain touch-target integrity.

## Elevation & Depth

In this system, depth is conveyed through **Light and Transparency** rather than traditional drop shadows.

- **Tonal Layering:** Surfaces use varying levels of "Pinewood Charcoal" to signify hierarchy. Darker surfaces sit in the background; slightly lighter or textured surfaces sit in the foreground.
- **Atmospheric Glow:** Interactive elements or featured products use a "Radial Amber Blur" behind them. This mimics the light passing through a SOM Universal sculpture.
- **Glassmorphism:** Navigation menus and overlays use a high-density backdrop blur (frosted effect) with a very thin, low-opacity gold border (0.5px) to suggest premium glass craftsmanship.
- **Zero Shadows:** Avoid standard black shadows. If depth is required, use a subtle "Glow Shadow" in a dark amber tint.

## Shapes

The shape language is primarily **Structural and Precise**, with organic softness reserved for the content (images/sculptures).

- **UI Elements:** Use "Soft" (0.25rem) corners for buttons and inputs. This provides just enough approachability without losing the architectural rigor of the design.
- **Image Containers:** Product photography should alternate between sharp rectangular frames and "Organic Masks" (circular or pebble-shaped) to represent annual growth rings.
- **Brackets:** Use bracket-style borders `[ EXPLORE ]` for high-level navigation, emphasizing the "Concept House" identity.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary CTAs are ghost-style with a thin gold border and amber text. On hover, the button should fill with a soft amber glow and transition text to black.
- **Cards:** Product cards are borderless with high-quality imagery that bleeds to the edges. Text overlays appear on hover using a bottom-to-top gradient fade.
- **Input Fields:** Minimalist underlines only. Active states are indicated by the underline turning from charcoal to amber.
- **Chips/Tags:** Small, all-caps text with a 1px solid border. Used for "Limited Edition" or "New Collection" badges.
- **Lists:** Clean, high-contrast list items separated by thin (0.5px) darkened dividers. Use a "Play" icon or "Chevron" in amber for interaction.
- **Navigation:** The header should be transparent, turning into a glassmorphic blur upon scroll. Links use high tracking and an amber underline on active/hover states.
